Getting the Parts

    Your first step is getting the required parts:

  • Case
  • Motherboard
  • CPU(Processor)
  • GPU(Graphics Card)
  • RAM(Memory)
  • Storage
  • Power Supply(PSU)

Installing the CPU

Take out your motherboard and place it on the Anti-Static Wrap it comes in. Then take out your CPU, pop open your CPU socket on the motherboard and match the arrow on the CPU to the socket, place it in, and close the socket. Most Intel CPUs come with stock cooling and thermal paste so I wouldnt worry about that for now.

Installing Motherboard and Power Supply

Put the motherboard with the CPU installed into your case. Follow the instructions on your motherboard manual and screw your motherboard into the approriate case holes. For the Power Supply look for a giant square spot , usually located in the back or towards the bottom of your case. Put it in and screw it in. Theres wires coming from the PSU, leave those alone for now.

Installing GPU

Look for a the PCIe slot on your motherboard( look to manual if you cant find it) it'll be a horizontal slot with a latch on the side. Pull the latch back and push your GPU into the slot and the latch should close automaticilly. Look at the wires from the PSU, there should be one(6 or 8 holes in a rectangle shape) that look like it fits into the GPU. If you cant find it look at the PSU manual and plug it into the GPU.

Installing Storage and Memory

Look for vertical sockets either next to the CPU or GPU. Thats where the RAM goes, its fairly easy to install, just pull the latches open and put the RAM sticks in. Depending on how much RAM you have , look to your motherboard to see which slots to use first, most motherboards have 4 RAM slots. For your storage, look to your case manual and find an empty bay where the storage is supposed to go, slide the Hard Drive or SSD in and screw it into place.

Plugging Everything In

Your motherboard needs to be connected to everything. The PSU has a cable to connect to the motherboard, consult the manual and plug it into the approriate slot. and find the PSU cable that connects to your storage and plug that in as well. Your case comes with wires for the power button, sleep button, etc. , the motherboard manual will tell you where these go, if you have trouble look for tiny plugs, these are usually very small pins. Look for a PSU cable that plugs into the GPU as well. Connect your storage wire to your motherboard too. Plugging in cables and wires depends on what hardware you used so the manuals are your best source here.

Booting and Installing Windows

Hit the power button after everything is plugged in and everything should turn on. If not, dont fret, just retrace your steps and double check everything.
