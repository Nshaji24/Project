When building a PC theres common errors you run into. Don't worry, most problems are easily solved. Below are a few of the most common. If your issue isnt listed here, feel free to contact me using the Contact option

    Common Problems

  1. Buying incompatible parts
  2. Installing CPU wrong
  3. Forgetting CPU power connection
  4. Not plugging in CPU cooler
  5. Leaving the plastic cover on CPU cooler
  6. Trying to slot RAM wrong
  7. Extra motherboard standoffs
  8. Forgetting a storage connection
  9. Placing the IO panel wrong
  10. Plugging monitor into motherboard

    Useful Tools

  • Anti-Static Arm Band: Prevents you from frying your parts with static electricity
  • Extra thermal paste: incase the CPU cooler doesnt have it applied or you want to apply more
  • Magnetic Screwdriver: very useful for when you have to screw/unscrew parts
  • PCPARTPICKER.COM

FIXES:

1) To check if parts are compatible you can use the website PCPARTPICKER.COM

2) All processors will have some kind of marking on one of the corners, on AMD chips it’s a gold arrow usually found on the bottom-left corner. That marking has to be lined up with the marking you’ll find on your motherboard’s CPU socket

3)Your CPU power connection is going to be found in the top left corner of your motherboard. Failing to hit that with a power connection will result in your build doing absolutely nothing when you try and power it on

4)You’re going to find your CPU cooler connection somewhere around the CPU socket on your motherboard
5)There is usually a little slip of transparent plastic that keeps your stock CPU cooler’s thermal paste from getting on everything, make sure its removed

6)When you’re installing your RAM, you want to use even pressure on either end until you hear the stick *click* into the slot. Also the motherboard tells you where the RAM should go.
7)The little metal pegs inside of your case that you mount your motherboard on are called standoffs These should be used only where they’re needed, any extra standoffs should always be removed from your case and kept aside

8)All drives outside of M.2 SSDs require 2 connections, 1 from your power supply and one from the drive to your motherboard

9)The front I/O connections can usually be found bundled together and shoved in the front of your case somewhere.

10)Track where its plugged in and make sure its towards the bottom of the case, usually where GPU rests.
